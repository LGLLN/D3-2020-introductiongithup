The Tree and the Reed

hello

"Well, little one,"
said a Tree to a Reed that was growing at its foot
"why do you not plant your feet deeply in the ground,
and raise your head boldly in the air as I do?"
"I am contented with my lot," said the Reed.
"I may not be so grand, but I think I am safer."
"Safe!" sneered the Tree.
"Who shall pluck me up by the roots or bow my head to the ground?"
But it soon had to repent of its boasting,
for a hurricane arose which tore it up from its roots,
and cast it a useless log on the ground,
while the little Reed,
bending to the force of the wind,
soon stood upright again when the storm had passed over.
Obscurity often brings safety.
